What is this

biota searches the parameter space of Flow-Lenia, a continuous cellular automaton where matter is conserved by construction. Rather than finding a single best creature, it fills a behavioral grid where each cell holds the most compact creature found with a particular phenotypic fingerprint. The result is a structured catalog of qualitatively distinct life-forms.

Each creature is a soliton: a stable, self-maintaining pattern that persists indefinitely given the right parameters. They move, rotate, and interact. Mass conservation is what makes them possible across a wide parameter range - without it, most settings produce explosion or collapse.

The search

MAP-Elites runs a loop: sample parameters, simulate a creature for 300 steps, measure its behavior, and insert it into the matching archive cell if it is better than whatever is already there. After thousands of rollouts the archive fills with creatures covering the behavioral space as broadly as possible.

Each run dispatches batches of simulations in parallel across a 3-node GPU cluster. One batch task evaluates B creatures as a single vectorized forward pass on one RTX 5060 Ti, returning B results. The driver owns the archive; workers are stateless.

Behavioral descriptors

Three scalars, each measured empirically from the rollout rather than derived from the parameter values. Together they index the archive's three axes.

velocity
Mean center-of-mass displacement per step over the trailing 50 steps. Separates drifters from stationary creatures.
gyradius
Mass-weighted RMS distance from the center of mass. Separates compact dots from spread-out rings and lattices.
spectral entropy
Shannon entropy of the radially-averaged FFT spectrum of the final state. Separates smooth blobs from sharp-edged structured creatures.
